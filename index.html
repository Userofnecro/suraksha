Know Your Rights

Voter Rights & Rules

Every citizen deserves to know their rights before stepping into the polling booth.

🗳️

Right to Vote

Every Indian citizen aged 18+ has the fundamental right to vote in elections. This right is protected under Article 326 of the Constitution and cannot be denied based on religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.

💡 Did You Know?

India has the world's largest democratic electorate — over 960 million registered voters as of 2024!

🔏

Secret Ballot

Your vote is completely secret. No one — not your employer, family member, or political party — can legally compel you to reveal how you voted. The EVM records only your vote, not your identity.

🚫

NOTA Option

If you find none of the candidates suitable, you can press NOTA (None Of The Above) on the EVM. Introduced by the Supreme Court in 2013, NOTA lets you register your dissatisfaction without spoiling your ballot.

💡 Did You Know?

You can vote using 12 different photo ID documents if you don't have a Voter ID — including Aadhaar, Passport, or Driving Licence!

🪪

Voter ID Rules

Your Voter ID (EPIC) is the primary document for voting. If lost, you can use Aadhaar Card, Passport, Driving Licence, PAN Card, MNREGA Job Card, Smart Card issued by RGI, or Pension document with photo.

📜

Model Code of Conduct

The Model Code of Conduct (MCC) kicks in from the date of election announcement. It prohibits parties from using government resources for campaigns, making promises that can't be kept, and inciting communal tensions.

⚖️

Penalties for Tampering

Voter intimidation, booth capturing, impersonation, and bribery are serious criminal offences under the Representation of the People Act, 1951. Penalties include imprisonment up to 5 years and fines. Report violations to 1950.

Yes, but you need to update your voter registration. Submit Form 8A (for shifting within the same constituency) or Form 6 (for a new constituency) at voters.eci.gov.in. Until your registration is updated, you must vote at your old polling booth. You cannot vote in two places — that is a criminal offence.

NOTA (None Of The Above) is the last option on every EVM. Use it when you genuinely feel none of the candidates deserve your vote. It registers your participation and dissatisfaction without benefiting any candidate. Note: even if NOTA gets the most votes, the candidate with the next highest votes still wins — but a high NOTA count sends a strong political message.

An Electronic Voting Machine (EVM) has two units: the Control Unit (with the polling officer) and the Balloting Unit (with the voter). When the officer enables it, you press the blue button next to your chosen candidate. The VVPAT printer attached to the EVM prints a paper slip showing your candidate's name and symbol for 7 seconds, then drops it into a sealed box — confirming your vote was recorded correctly.

On polling day itself, if your name is absent, you cannot vote — so always verify your registration well before election day. Register early using Form 6 at voters.eci.gov.in.
